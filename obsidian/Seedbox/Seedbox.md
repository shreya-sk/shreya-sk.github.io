# Seedbox

This is where ideas live **before they're ready**.

A seed is a thought that's still forming — a half-understood concept, a hunch, a "what if" that needs more reading before it becomes a real note. Seeds get planted here with no pressure to be complete, correct, or even coherent.

When a seed grows up — when I actually understand the thing — it graduates out of this folder and into the main notes (DevOps, Kong, wherever it belongs). Until then, expect fragments.

> [!note] Why keep them public?
> Because watching an idea develop is half the fun. The messy middle is the honest part.
