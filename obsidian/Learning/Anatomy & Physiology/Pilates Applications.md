# Pilates Applications — NASM A&P

> Practical connections between NASM anatomy & physiology content and Pilates instruction. For Tensegrity Cert IV RPL evidence — SISFFIT047.

---

## 1. Breathing Has Real Physiology Behind It

- [[Cardiorespiratory System/Cardiorespiratory System#Respiratory Muscles|Diaphragm + intercostals]] are primary respiratory muscles
- Proper breathing = better O₂ delivery + thoracic mobility + core stability
- Respiratory pump improves venous return (circulation back to heart)
- Poor breathing patterns → restricted spine/ribs, anxiety, fatigue
- Teaching breath patterns = genuine physiological benefit for clients

**The Pilates breath** (lateral thoracic): rib cage expands side-to-side and posteriorly on inhale, activates intercostals maximally, allows TVA to stay engaged throughout.

**Classical breath cue:** Inhale to prepare → exhale to exert effort. The exhalation engages the deep abdominals via the diaphragm-TVA-pelvic floor connection (the "core canister").

---

## 2. Cueing Works Through the Nervous System

- Every verbal cue = input to CNS shaping motor output
- Clear, simple cues respect the [[Nervous System/Nervous System#Motor Skill Learning — Three Stages|three stages of motor learning]]
- Over-cueing clients in the cognitive stage = information overload
- Autonomous clients need progression challenges
- Tactile cues can bypass cognitive processing when verbal cues fail (e.g., hand on ribcage to guide breath)

| Client Stage | Cueing Strategy |
|---|---|
| Cognitive (new) | One cue at a time, break movement into small steps |
| Associative (getting it) | Refine + consistent feedback |
| Autonomous (mastered) | Add complexity, novel variations, less cueing |

## 3. Kinetic Chain = Foundation of Posture Assessment

- Dysfunction at one joint causes cascading compensations
- Collapsed arch → hip drop → shoulder tension (see [[Kinetic Chain/Kinetic Chain#Regional Interdependence — The Domino Effect|Regional Interdependence]])
- This is WHY Pilates assesses whole-body posture, not isolated areas
- Helps identify root cause, not just symptoms

**Pilates postural assessment reference:**

| Region | Look for | Likely imbalance |
|---|---|---|
| Foot | Pronation (arch collapse) | Tight peroneals, weak tibialis posterior |
| Knee | Valgus (inward) | Weak glute med, tight TFL |
| Pelvis | Anterior tilt | Tight hip flexors, weak glutes/abs |
| Lumbar | Excessive lordosis | Tight hip flexors, inhibited TVA |
| Thoracic | Kyphosis | Tight pec major/minor, weak mid-traps |
| Cervical | Forward head | Tight SCM/scalenes, weak deep neck flexors |
| Scapulae | Protraction | Weak rhomboids/middle traps |

## 4. Train All Three Planes of Motion

- Most daily movement is [[Biomechanics & Kinesiology/Biomechanics & Kinesiology#Sagittal Plane Movements (Flexion/Extension)|sagittal]] (forward-back)
- Programs must include [[Biomechanics & Kinesiology/Biomechanics & Kinesiology#Frontal Plane Movements|frontal]] and [[Biomechanics & Kinesiology/Biomechanics & Kinesiology#Transverse Plane Movements|transverse]] work
- Prevents imbalances and real-world injury patterns
- Classical Pilates repertoire naturally includes this:

| Plane | Examples in Pilates |
|---|---|
| Sagittal | Roll-up, spine stretch, hip flexion/extension in footwork |
| Frontal | Side-lying leg series, side bend, lateral breathing |
| Transverse | Spine twist, saw, oblique work, hip rotation |
| Multiplanar | Swimming, snake, advanced combination exercises |

## 5. Full Muscle Action Spectrum

- [[Biomechanics & Kinesiology/Biomechanics & Kinesiology#The Full Spectrum|Eccentric and isometric]] = critical for joint health
- Pilates naturally emphasises this — slow, controlled, held positions
- Reformer springs deliver resistance in all three phases:
    - **Concentric** — pushing/pulling carriage away
    - **Eccentric** — controlling return of carriage (springs pull back)
    - **Isometric** — holding position under spring load
- This is one of Pilates' key advantages over traditional weight training

---

## 6. Proprioception Is Trainable

- [[Nervous System/Nervous System#Proprioception|Proprioception]] = body's sense of its own position without looking
- Single-leg work, balance challenges, unfamiliar patterns train the nervous system
- Explains why Pilates improves balance in older adults
- Progression: stable surface → slightly unstable (Pilates arc, foam roller) → fully unstable

---

## 7. Rest Intervals Shape Adaptation

Understanding [[Bioenergetics & Energy Systems/Bioenergetics & Energy Systems#Programming by Energy System|energy systems]] = knowing why to rest 30 sec vs 3 min:

| Class style | Energy system | Adaptation |
|---|---|---|
| Classical flows (minimal rest) | Glycolytic + oxidative | Muscular endurance, cardio fitness |
| Heavy spring work + full rest (2-5 min) | ATP-PCr | Power, strength |
| Long sustained holds | Oxidative + glycolytic | Postural endurance |

---

## 8. Muscle Spindle & GTO Knowledge = Better Stretching

- [[Nervous System/Nervous System#Mechanoreceptors — The Three Critical Types|Muscle spindles]] → stretch reflex — rapid bouncing triggers protective contraction
- [[Nervous System/Nervous System#Mechanoreceptors — The Three Critical Types|GTOs]] → sense tension → trigger relaxation with held stretch
- Held stretches activate GTO → muscle relaxes deeper
- **Slow, sustained stretches work better than bouncing** — this is the science behind classical stretch cues

---

## 9. Stabiliser Muscles Matter Most

- Deep muscles ([[Muscular System/Muscular System#Feed Forward Activation|TVA, multifidus]], rotator cuff) = unsung heroes
- Pilates emphasises these from Day 1
- Feed-forward activation is trained with precise cueing — "engage your centre before you move"
- Sets Pilates apart from pure cardio or weight training

**The Core Canister:**

| Muscle | Role in stability |
|---|---|
| TVA (transversus abdominis) | Wraps trunk like a corset, creates intra-abdominal pressure |
| Multifidus | Deep spinal stabiliser, vertebra-by-vertebra control |
| Pelvic floor | Co-activates with TVA, closes canister from below |
| Diaphragm | Closes canister from above, coordinates with breath |

---

## 10. Bone Health Through Weight-Bearing

- [[Skeletal System/Skeletal System#Exercise & Bone Health|Weight-bearing exercise]] = best stimulus for bone density
- Reformer work IS weight-bearing (resistance against springs)
- Mat Pilates = weight-bearing through gravity
- Especially important for **female clients 40+** as osteoporosis risk rises after 50
- Safer than high-impact but delivers adequate bone stimulus
